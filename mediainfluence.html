Media Influence & Brain Tricks

How Ads Make Us Crave Stuff We Didn’t Even Think About 5 Minutes Ago

Have you noticed how you crave fries right after seeing an ad? Or suddenly want chips because an influencer opened a packet in slow motion?

That’s not random. That’s design.

How Ads Sneak Into Your Brain

Media knows exactly how to push your buttons:

  • Red and yellow make you hungry
  • Crispy sound effects hit the brain’s pleasure center
  • Slow-motion food shots make everything look irresistible
  • “Healthy” labels make us drop our guard
  • Seeing a celebrity eat something makes us trust it instantly

We don’t stand a chance sometimes. Our brains love shortcuts, and ads know how to exploit them.

We’re Not Craving the Food — We’re Craving the Feeling

Ads don’t sell food. They sell:

  • Comfort
  • Fun
  • Childhood memories
  • “You deserve this”
  • “Everyone’s eating it”
  • “This will make your day better”

And guess what? It works.

Why Resisting Feels Impossible

Because the whole system is designed to beat your willpower.

  • Bright colors
  • Perfect crunch
  • Immediate dopamine
  • Convenience
  • Emotional messaging

Nothing about this is “lack of discipline.” It’s a setup. But once you see the setup, you don’t fall for it as easily.

Breaking the Spell (Without Being Extreme)

Here’s some real-life stuff that actually works:

  • If an ad triggers a craving → tell yourself “that’s just the ad talking”
  • Don’t shop while hungry
  • Unfollow food content that influences you too much
  • Check ingredients instead of believing the front label
  • Ask: “If I never saw this ad, would I want this right now?”

You don’t need to quit media. You just need to wake up to how it shapes your appetite.
